# Major Funder Profiles

Quick reference for common grant funders and their priorities.

## Federal Funders

### National Science Foundation (NSF)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Focus** | Basic research in science and engineering |
| **Award Range** | $100K - $10M+ (varies by program) |
| **Duration** | 1-5 years |
| **Success Rate** | ~25% overall |
| **Review Criteria** | Intellectual Merit, Broader Impacts |

**Key Programs for Creative/Technical Work:**
- CISE (Computer & Information Science)
- SBE (Social, Behavioral & Economic Sciences)
- EHR (Education & Human Resources)
- Convergence Accelerator

**Tips:**
- Broader Impacts are weighted equally with Intellectual Merit
- Include education/training components
- Emphasize novelty and transformative potential
- Use clear, jargon-free language

---

### National Endowment for the Arts (NEA)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Focus** | Arts projects with public benefit |
| **Award Range** | $10K - $100K (most are $10K-$25K) |
| **Duration** | 1-2 years typically |
| **Match Required** | 1:1 match typically required |
| **Success Rate** | ~40-50% |

**Key Programs:**
- Grants for Arts Projects
- Our Town (arts + community development)
- Research Grants in the Arts

**Tips:**
- Strong emphasis on public access and benefit
- Demonstrate community engagement
- Show organizational capacity
- Matching funds must be secured

---

### National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Focus** | Humanities research, education, preservation |
| **Award Range** | $5K - $350K+ |
| **Duration** | 1-3 years |
| **Success Rate** | 15-30% (varies by program) |

**Key Programs:**
- Fellowships (individual research)
- Public Humanities Projects
- Digital Humanities Advancement

**Tips:**
- Strong humanities question required

### National Institutes of Health (NIH)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Focus** | Biomedical and public health research |
| **Award Range** | $75K - $500K+ annually |
| **Duration** | 1-5 years |
| **Success Rate** | ~20% for new investigators |

**Key Programs:**
- R01 (major research grant)
- R21 (exploratory/developmental)
- R03 (small grant)
- K awards (career development)

**Tips:**
- Preliminary data almost always required
- Significance, Innovation, Approach, Investigators, Environment
- Resubmissions often succeed
- Work with your grants office

### Research Strategy

1. **Start with problem, not funder**
   - What are you trying to do?
   - Who cares about this issue?

2. **Look at who funded similar work**
   - Check acknowledgments in papers
   - Look at project websites
   - Review annual reports

3. **Assess fit before applying**
   - Mission alignment critical
   - Check geographic restrictions
   - Verify eligibility
   - Review past grants

4. **Build relationships**
   - Attend funder events
   - Connect through program officers
   - Leverage board connections
