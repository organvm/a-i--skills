# Workshop Activity Library

A collection of engagement activities for workshops and presentations.

## Icebreakers & Openers

### Quick Starters (5-10 minutes)

#### Two Truths and a Lie (Professional Edition)
**Time:** 5-10 min | **Size:** 5-20 | **Energy:** Medium

Each participant shares three statements about their professional experience—two true, one false. Others guess the lie.

**Variations:**
- Topic-specific (all statements about [subject])
- Speed round (30 seconds per person)
- Team competition

---

#### Rose, Thorn, Bud
**Time:** 5-10 min | **Size:** Any | **Energy:** Low

Share one of each:
- **Rose:** Something positive (highlight, success)
- **Thorn:** A challenge or difficulty
- **Bud:** Something you're looking forward to

**Use for:** Check-ins, mood assessment, framing discussions

---

#### One Word Check-In
**Time:** 2-5 min | **Size:** Any | **Energy:** Low

Each person shares one word describing how they feel or their expectation.

**Variations:**
- Two words
- One word + brief explanation
- Themed word (related to topic)

---

### Energizers (5-15 minutes)

#### Stand Up If...
**Time:** 5 min | **Size:** Any | **Energy:** Medium

Facilitator calls statements: "Stand up if you've [done X]"
Builds connection through shared experiences.

**Example prompts:**
- "Stand up if you've worked on a project like this"
- "Stand up if you learned something new this week"
- "Stand up if you prefer morning coffee over tea"

---

#### This or That
**Time:** 5 min | **Size:** Any | **Energy:** Medium

Binary choices, participants move to sides of room (or virtual poll).

**Professional examples:**
- Planning vs. improvising
- Individual vs. team work
- Big picture vs. details

---

## Discussion Formats

### Think-Pair-Share
**Time:** 10-15 min | **Size:** Any | **Energy:** Medium

1. **Think:** Individual reflection (2-3 min)
2. **Pair:** Discuss with partner (3-5 min)
3. **Share:** Report to larger group (5 min)

**Use for:** Processing new information, generating ideas, safe participation

---

### Fishbowl
**Time:** 20-30 min | **Size:** 12+ | **Energy:** Medium

```
Outer circle (observers)
    ┌─────────────┐
    │  ○   ○   ○  │
    │    Inner    │
    │   circle    │
    │ (discussers)│
    │  ○   ○   ○  │
    └─────────────┘
```

- Inner circle (4-6) discusses topic
- Outer circle observes
- Rotate people in/out
- Optional: "hot seat" for observers to join temporarily

**Use for:** Modeling discussion, multiple perspectives, deeper exploration

---

### Gallery Walk
**Time:** 15-25 min | **Size:** 8-30 | **Energy:** Medium-High

1. Post content/questions around room (4-8 stations)
2. Small groups rotate through stations
3. Groups add comments, ideas, or feedback
4. Debrief highlights

**Station types:**
- Questions to answer
- Case studies to analyze
- Brainstorm topics
- Review others' work

---

### World Café
**Time:** 30-60 min | **Size:** 12-50 | **Energy:** Medium

- Tables of 4-6 with different topics
- 3-4 rounds of 10-15 minutes each
- One person stays as "host," others rotate
- Build on previous groups' ideas

**Use for:** Large-scale brainstorming, cross-pollination of ideas

---

## Brainstorming Activities

### Brainwriting (6-3-5 Method)
**Time:** 20-30 min | **Size:** 6-24 | **Energy:** Low

- 6 people per group
- Each writes 3 ideas in 5 minutes
- Pass paper to next person
- Build on or add new ideas
- Repeat 6 times

**Use for:** Inclusive idea generation, avoiding groupthink

---

### Crazy 8s
**Time:** 10-15 min | **Size:** Any | **Energy:** Medium

- Fold paper into 8 sections
- 1 minute per section
- Sketch 8 different ideas
- Forces rapid, varied thinking

**Use for:** Design thinking, visual ideas, breaking fixation

---

### SCAMPER
**Time:** 20-30 min | **Size:** Any | **Energy:** Medium

Apply each lens to existing solution:
- **S**ubstitute: What could be swapped?
- **C**ombine: What could be merged?
- **A**dapt: What could be adjusted?
- **M**odify: What could be changed?
- **P**ut to other use: Different application?
- **E**liminate: What's unnecessary?
- **R**everse: What if opposite?

## Learning Activities

### Jigsaw
**Time:** 30-45 min | **Size:** 12-30 | **Energy:** Medium

1. Divide topic into 4-5 parts
2. Form "expert groups" (one part each)
3. Expert groups study their section
4. Reform into "teaching groups" (one expert each)
5. Experts teach their section to teaching group

**Use for:** Large content coverage, peer teaching, engagement

---

### Case Study Analysis
**Time:** 20-45 min | **Size:** Any | **Energy:** Medium

Provide scenario with:
- Context and background
- Challenge or decision point
- Data/constraints
- Discussion questions

**Structure:**
1. Read case (5-10 min)
2. Small group discussion (15-20 min)
3. Report out (10-15 min)

---

### Role Play / Simulation
**Time:** 20-40 min | **Size:** 4-20 | **Energy:** High

- Assign roles with different perspectives
- Present scenario requiring interaction
- Debrief what happened and why

**Use for:** Soft skills, negotiations, understanding perspectives

---

## Feedback & Reflection

### Plus/Delta
**Time:** 5-10 min | **Size:** Any | **Energy:** Low

```
+──────────────┬──────────────Δ
│   Positives  │  Changes     │
│   (keep)     │  (improve)   │
├──────────────┼──────────────┤
│              │              │
└──────────────┴──────────────┘
```

**Use for:** Session feedback, project retrospectives, quick evaluation

---

### Start, Stop, Continue
**Time:** 10-15 min | **Size:** Any | **Energy:** Low

Gather input on:
- **Start:** What should we begin doing?
- **Stop:** What should we stop doing?
- **Continue:** What's working well?

---

### One-Minute Paper
**Time:** 5 min | **Size:** Any | **Energy:** Low

At session end, write for 1 minute:
- What was the most important thing you learned?
- What question remains unanswered?

**Use for:** Assessing understanding, identifying gaps

---

### Muddiest Point
**Time:** 5 min | **Size:** Any | **Energy:** Low

"What was the muddiest (least clear) point in today's session?"

**Use for:** Identifying confusion, informing follow-up

## Virtual Adaptations

### For All Activities

| In-Person | Virtual Equivalent |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Stand up | Reactions, polls |
| Move to corners | Breakout rooms by choice |
| Gallery walk | Miro/Mural boards, shared docs |
| Pair discussion | Breakout rooms (2 people) |
| Show of hands | Polls, chat response |
| Pass paper | Shared document, comment |
| Physical voting | Online polls, emoji |

### Virtual-Specific Tools

| Tool | Best For |
|------|----------|
| Polls (Zoom, Slido) | Quick votes, temperature checks |
| Chat | Parallel input, "waterfall" technique |
| Breakout rooms | Small group discussion |
| Whiteboard (Miro, Mural) | Collaborative brainstorming |
| Shared docs | Collaborative writing, brainwriting |

### Virtual Engagement Tips

- Shorter activities (attention spans lower)
- More frequent interaction (every 3-5 min)
- Use chat actively
- Call on people by name
- Use visual timers
- Take more breaks

## Activity Selection Guide

### By Energy Level Needed

| Energy | Activities |
|--------|------------|
| High (boost energy) | Stand Up If, Crazy 8s, Role Play |
| Medium | Think-Pair-Share, Gallery Walk |
| Low (calm focus) | One Word, Reflection, Writing |

### By Time Available

| Time | Activities |
|------|------------|
| 5-10 min | Icebreakers, Quick Reflection |
| 15-20 min | Think-Pair-Share, Plus/Delta |
| 30+ min | Jigsaw, World Café, Role Play |

### By Group Size

| Size | Activities |
|------|------------|
| Small (5-10) | Any; more intimate discussion |
| Medium (10-25) | Gallery Walk, Case Study, Jigsaw |
| Large (25+) | World Café, Brainwriting, Polls |

### By Learning Goal

| Goal | Activities |
|------|------------|
| Generate ideas | Brainwriting, Crazy 8s, SCAMPER |
| Build connections | Icebreakers, Think-Pair-Share |
| Deep understanding | Jigsaw, Case Study, Role Play |
| Gather feedback | Plus/Delta, One-Minute Paper |
| Apply learning | Case Study, Role Play, Simulation |
