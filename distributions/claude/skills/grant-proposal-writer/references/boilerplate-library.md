# Grant Boilerplate Library

Reusable sections for grant proposals. Customize each block with project-specific details before including in a submission.

---

## Organizational Description

### Research University

```
[Institution Name] is a [public/private] research university founded in [year],
serving approximately [N] students across [N] colleges and schools. Classified
as an R1 doctoral university (Carnegie Classification), [Institution] generates
over $[N] million annually in sponsored research and employs [N] faculty members.
The university holds accreditation from [accrediting body] and is recognized for
strengths in [relevant disciplines].
```

### Nonprofit Organization

```
[Organization Name] is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit established in [year] with a
mission to [mission statement]. Based in [location], [Organization] serves
approximately [N] individuals annually through [programs/services]. Our annual
operating budget is $[N], supported by [funding sources]. We maintain a [N]-member
staff and collaborate with [N] partner organizations across [geographic area].
```

### Small Business / Independent Researcher

```
[Business/Name] is a [type of entity] specializing in [domain]. Founded in [year],
we have completed [N] projects for clients including [notable examples]. Our team
of [N] professionals brings [N] combined years of experience in [relevant fields].
```

## Qualifications and Capacity

### Principal Investigator

```
[Name] ([title]) brings [N] years of experience in [field]. [He/She/They] has
published [N] peer-reviewed articles in [journals], received $[N] in prior
funding from [agencies], and supervised [N] graduate students. [Name]'s work
on [prior project] demonstrated [relevant outcome], establishing the foundation
for the proposed research. [Name] will devote [N]% effort to this project.
```

### Team Qualifications Summary

```
The project team combines expertise in [Field A], [Field B], and [Field C].
Together, the team brings [N] years of combined experience and has previously
collaborated on [describe prior joint work]. The team's complementary skills
ensure comprehensive coverage of the project's [technical/programmatic/creative]
requirements.
```

### Institutional Resources

```
[Institution] provides a supportive environment for the proposed work, including:
- [Facility/lab type] with [specific capabilities]
- Dedicated [computing/administrative/library] support
- Access to [relevant collections, datasets, populations]
- [Office of Sponsored Programs / grants office] for compliance and reporting
- [IRB/IACUC/other oversight body] for [human subjects/animal research] review
```

## Broader Impacts / Significance

### Education and Training

```
This project will directly support the training of [N] [graduate students /
postdoctoral researchers / undergraduate assistants], providing hands-on
experience in [methods/tools]. Participants will develop skills in [specific
competencies] and will be mentored in [professional development areas]. We
expect trainees to co-author [N] publications and present at [N] conferences.
```

### Diversity and Inclusion

```
We are committed to broadening participation in [field]. Our recruitment plan
targets [underrepresented groups] through partnerships with [specific programs,
institutions, or organizations]. The PI has a demonstrated record of mentoring
diverse trainees: [brief evidence]. We will track demographic data and report
outcomes related to diversity in annual reports to [funder].
```

### Public Engagement and Dissemination

```
Results will be disseminated through: (1) peer-reviewed publications in open-access
journals, (2) presentations at national and international conferences including
[conference names], (3) a project website with publicly accessible resources,
and (4) [community workshops / public lectures / media engagement]. All data and
code will be deposited in [repository name] under [license type].
```

## Evaluation and Assessment

### Process Evaluation

```
We will track implementation fidelity through [activity logs, attendance records,
milestone tracking]. The Project Director will conduct [monthly/quarterly] reviews
comparing planned vs. actual activities. Deviations from the work plan will be
documented with corrective actions.
```

### Outcome Evaluation

```
Program outcomes will be measured using [pre/post surveys, standardized assessments,
performance metrics]. Data will be collected at [timepoints] and analyzed using
[methods]. We will compare outcomes against baseline measures established in
[month/year]. An external evaluator, [Name/TBD], will provide independent
assessment of outcomes on [annual/semi-annual] basis.
```

### Logic Model Template

```
Inputs → Activities → Outputs → Short-term Outcomes → Long-term Outcomes

Inputs:         Funding, personnel, facilities, partnerships
Activities:     [Program activities, research tasks]
Outputs:        [Number of participants served, products created]
Short-term:     [Knowledge gained, skills developed, attitudes changed]
Long-term:      [Behavioral change, systemic impact, sustainability]
```

## Sustainability and Future Plans

### Revenue Continuation

```
Beyond the grant period, the project will sustain operations through [revenue
sources: fees for service, licensing, institutional commitment, follow-on grants].
We have already initiated conversations with [partners/funders] regarding
continued support, and [Institution] has committed to [specific ongoing
contribution]. The infrastructure developed during this project will reduce
future costs by approximately [N]%.
```

### Institutionalization

```
[Institution] has committed to integrating [program/tool/method] into its ongoing
operations upon completion of the grant period. This includes [specific commitments:
dedicated staff line, incorporation into curriculum, continued server hosting].
A letter of support from [Provost/Director/Dean] confirming this commitment is
included in the appendix.
```

## Data Management

### Data Management Plan

```
All data generated by this project will be managed in accordance with [funder]
policies and [institutional] guidelines. Primary data will be stored on
[secure servers / institutional storage] with daily backups. De-identified
datasets will be deposited in [repository name] within [timeframe] of
publication. Data will be preserved for a minimum of [N] years following
project completion. Personally identifiable information will be protected
in compliance with [IRB protocol / FERPA / HIPAA / GDPR] and stored
separately from research data.
```

---

## Usage Notes

- Replace all bracketed placeholders before including in a proposal
- Adjust tone to match the funder (federal proposals are more formal; foundation proposals can be warmer)
- Always verify that boilerplate statistics (enrollment, budget, publication counts) are current
- Have your grants office review institutional descriptions for accuracy
- Tailor broader impacts and sustainability sections to each funder's priorities
