# Reality TV Analytical Terms Glossary

## Production Terms

**B-roll**: Supplementary footage used to cover edits or add visual interest (establishing shots, nature, reactions).

**Confessional/Diary Room/ITM**: Interview segments where contestants speak directly to camera, often recorded separately from events.

**Cold Open**: Pre-credits sequence, often a dramatic moment from later in the episode.

**Frankenbite**: Audio edited together from different takes/contexts to create new sentences or meanings.

**Fourth Wall**: The conceptual barrier between performers and audience; "breaking" it means acknowledging the camera/show.

**OTF (On-The-Fly)**: Confessional recorded immediately after an event, capturing raw emotion.

**Package**: Pre-produced segment combining footage, interviews, and music to tell a contained story.

**Pickup Interview**: Additional confessional recorded after initial filming to clarify storylines.

**Softening**: Editing techniques to make a contestant appear more sympathetic.

**Story Producer**: Producer responsible for shaping raw footage into compelling narratives.

---

## Edit Analysis Terms

**Edit/Editing Pattern**: The overall narrative framing a contestant receives across an episode or season.

**Invisible Edit**: When a contestant receives little screen time or characterization. Often (not always) signals early elimination.

**Positive/Negative Edit**: Whether the editing presents a contestant sympathetically or unsympathetically.

**Purple Edit**: Extremely minimal screen time, often for contestants eliminated early or deemed uninteresting.

**Winner Edit**: Pattern of content distribution believed to indicate eventual winner (consistent positive presence, strategic insight, growth arc).

**Decoy Edit**: Edit designed to make viewer believe someone might win when they won't.

**Villain Edit**: Consistent negative framing through unflattering footage, ominous music, and "caught" moments.

**Growth Edit**: Narrative showing positive character development over the season.

**Downfall Edit**: Increasing negative content leading to elimination, often for previously heroic figures.

**Boot Episode**: The episode in which a contestant is eliminated. Eliminated players typically spike in content.

---

## Narrative Structure Terms

**Arc**: The overall trajectory of a contestant's story across a season.

**A-Plot/B-Plot**: Primary and secondary storylines within an episode.

**Beat**: Individual story moment within larger narrative.

**Cliffhanger**: Unresolved tension at episode's end designed to bring viewers back.

**Payoff**: Resolution of previously established narrative thread.

**Plant**: Early story element intended to pay off later.

**Setup**: Establishing information that will become relevant later.

**Callbacks**: References to earlier moments in the season.

---

## Competition-Specific Terms

**Alliance**: Group of contestants working together strategically.

**Blindside**: Elimination the target didn't anticipate.

**Backdoor**: (Big Brother) Nominating replacement after veto to eliminate true target.

**Flip**: Changing vote/allegiance unexpectedly.

**Floater**: Contestant without solid alliance loyalty.

**Goat**: Contestant brought to finale because they're easy to beat.

**Immunity**: Protection from elimination.

**Jury Management**: Strategic consideration of how eliminated players will vote in finale.

**Meat Shield**: Keeping bigger threats as targets ahead of yourself.

**Pagonging**: Systematically eliminating one alliance/tribe before turning on each other.

**Power Couple/Showmance**: Romantic pair with strategic implications.

**Social Game**: Relationships and likability as strategic asset.

**Strategic Game**: Challenge wins, vote manipulation, and tactical moves.

---

## Audience/Fan Terms

**Edgic**: (Edit + Logic) Fan methodology analyzing edit patterns to predict winner.

**Casual Viewer**: Audience member who watches without deeper analysis.

**Stan**: Devoted fan of specific contestant or show.

**Tea/Spillage**: Behind-the-scenes information or gossip.

**Receipts**: Evidence supporting a claim (usually about contestant behavior).

---

## Academic/Critical Terms

**Authenticity Performance**: Acting "real" for cameras; the paradox of performed naturalness.

**Constructed Reality**: The produced nature of "unscripted" television.

**Gamedoc**: Documentary-style competition format.

**Parasocial Relationship**: One-sided emotional connection viewers develop with TV personalities.

**Spectacle of Surveillance**: Foucauldian analysis of reality TV's panoptic dynamics.

**Mediated Self**: How identity is constructed through camera/editing apparatus.

**Carnivalesque**: Moments where normal social rules are suspended (borrowed from Bakhtin).

**Seriality**: How ongoing narratives create viewer commitment across episodes/seasons.

---

## Analytical Questions

When analyzing an episode or season, consider:

1. **Distribution**: Who gets the most confessional time? Why?
2. **Framing**: How are contestants introduced? What music accompanies them?
3. **Context**: What's omitted? What's shown out of order?
4. **Function**: What narrative purpose does each scene serve?
5. **Ideology**: What values does the show reward? Punish?
6. **Audience**: Who is the implied viewer? What assumptions are made?
7. **Commerce**: How do edit choices serve the show's business interests?
